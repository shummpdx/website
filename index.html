[About Me]

I'm Sean. I'm a graduate student at Portland State University studying computer science. I'm interested in DevOps and Cloud Computing. I am currently gaining leadership experience as a Lead Technical Writer for the Computer Action Team (CAT).

[Experience]

My first year with the CAT was spent working the help desk responding to tickets and ensuring the computer labs were functioning properly. On Friday evenings, we would attend weekly lectures about not only the infrastructure within MCECS, but also, user services, platform specific user/admin roles (Windows/Linux), and networking concepts.

The following summer I took on the role of a mentor where I updated many of the presentations that would be given to new volunteers to help through the onboarding process. I created homework assignments and speaking notes. In the fall I led 8 2-hour virtual orientation sessions with a group of 6 volunteers ensuring that they felt comfortable navigating the many tools and resoucers we offer.

As a lead to the technical writing team, I mentor volunteers in developing internal and user facing documentation for other volunteers, students, faculty, and staff at MCECS. I manage two full-scale MediaWikis and two full-scale Wordpress solutions. Along with weekly team meetings, I gain a lot of experience with public speaking by delivering presentations about the tools we use to perspective volunteers. I created resources to guide volunteers on the tools and knowledge needed to join the other various teams within the CAT.

In my free time I enjoy learning about cloud computing concepts and automation via IaC. I also have a raspberry pi home server to learn about new technologies and practice new skills such as Docker container deployment/troubleshooting. It currently serves as a centrailzed storage via NAS and These associted pages are hosted and maintained with AWS in conjunction with Terraform.
